The Infinite Legend

The Prancing Horse

The Spirit of Baracca

In 1923, Enzo Ferrari met Countess Paolina, mother of Count Francesco Baracca, an Italian air force ace. She told him, "Ferrari, put my son's prancing horse on your cars. It will bring you luck."

The horse was, and still is, black, but Enzo added the canary yellow background — the color of his birthplace, Modena. Since then, the Cavallino Rampante has become the most recognized symbol of excellence in the world.

1947

THE 125 S: WHERE IT ALL BEGAN

On March 12, 1947, Enzo Ferrari ignited the engine of the 125 S, the very first car to bear his name. With its revolutionary 1.5-liter V12 engine designed by Gioachino Colombo, it wasn't just a car; it was a pure declaration of war on the racing establishment.

From the streets of Maranello to the circuits of the world, this crimson machine set in motion a legacy of passion, performance, and uncompromising perfection that would dominate the next century.

Decades of Dominance

The F1 Golden Eras

1975 – 1977

The Lauda Supremacy

Niki Lauda brought technical precision and cold calculation to Maranello. Paired with the revolutionary 312T featuring a transverse gearbox, Lauda dominated the grid, delivering two World Drivers' Championships and revitalizing Scuderia Ferrari's terrifying reputation.

2000 – 2004

The Dream Team

Michael Schumacher, Jean Todt, and Ross Brawn orchestrated the most dominant era in Formula 1 history. Schumacher secured five consecutive Drivers' titles, painting the globe red and turning the F2004 into an immortal myth of speed and reliability.
